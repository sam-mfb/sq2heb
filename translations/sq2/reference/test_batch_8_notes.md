# Test Batch 8.agilogic - Translation Notes

## Message 1
**Original:** "You head back down to the lower level. Unfortunately, you are met by an armed guard. You have been quite a headache. To make it up to them, you are strung between two hovercraft. On the command \"GO\", life leaves you (in two different directions)."

**Translation:** "אתה יורד חזרה לקומה התחתונה. לצערך, שומר חמוש מחכה לך. היית כאב ראש לא קטן. כדי לפצות אותם, אתה נמתח בין שתי רחפות. לפקודה \"קדימה\", החיים עוזבים אותך (בשני כיוונים שונים)."

**Decision:** This is a death message with dark humor. Translated "hovercraft" as "רחפות" (plural of "רחפת") - consistent with SQ1's usage of "רחפת" for hovering vehicles. "You have been quite a headache" became "היית כאב ראש לא קטן" (literally "you were no small headache") - more idiomatic Hebrew. The sarcastic tone is maintained with "כדי לפצות אותם" (to make it up to them). The parenthetical joke "(in two different directions)" maintains the dark humor: "(בשני כיוונים שונים)". "GO" command translated as "קדימה" (forward/go).

**Term verification:**
- guard → שומר (SQ1 reference: "guard" = "שומר")
- hovercraft → רחפת (SQ1 reference: "hover platform" = "משטח רחפת")

## Message 2
**Original:** "After surviving to this point, you are still prone to acts typical of the mentally absent."

**Translation:** "אחרי ששרדת עד לשלב הזה, אתה עדיין נוטה למעשים טיפוסיים לנעדרי-שפיות."

**Decision:** This is classic Space Quest sarcasm - telling the player they're an idiot for dying. "Mentally absent" is a polite way of saying "stupid" - translated as "נעדרי-שפיות" (absent of sanity/wit), which maintains the sarcastic politeness. "Prone to acts" = "נוטה למעשים" (tends toward acts). The Hebrew flows naturally while preserving the condescending tone.

## Message 3
**Original:** "Ugghh!"

**Translation:** "אוווף!"

**Decision:** Death/pain sound effect. Hebrew equivalent "אוווף" captures the guttural sound of pain/death. Alternative could be "אוווך" or "אוי", but "אוווף" best matches the English sound's intensity.

## Message 4

**Translation:** "אתה לא יכול לעשות את זה מפה."

**Decision:** Standard adventure game message. Simple, direct translation. "מפה" (from here) is casual Israeli Hebrew, appropriate for the game's tone.

## Message 5
**Original:** "You are at some kind of landing platform. The guards are transferring you to another mode of transportation for the remainder of the trip to the mine."

**Translation:** "אתה נמצא באיזשהו משטח נחיתה. השומרים מעבירים אותך לאמצעי תחבורה אחר בשביל שארית הנסיעה למכרה."

**Decision:** Narrative scene description. "Landing platform" = "משטח נחיתה" (consistent with SQ1's "משטח" for platform). "Guards" = "שומרים" (plural). "Mode of transportation" = "אמצעי תחבורה" (standard Hebrew term). "Remainder" = "שארית" (remnant/remainder). "Mine" = "מכרה" (mining site).

**Term verification:**
- platform → משטח (SQ1: "platform" = "משטח")
- guard → שומר (SQ1: "guard" = "שומר")

## Message 6
**Original:** "You are on a landing platform. A shuttle sits quietly nearby. You see lush growth all around."

**Translation:** "אתה נמצא על משטח נחיתה. רכב הסעות יושב בשקט בקרבת מקום. אתה רואה צמחייה שופעת מסביבך."

**Decision:** Peaceful scene description. "Shuttle" = "רכב הסעות" (consistent with SQ1: "shuttle craft" = "רכב הסעות"). "Sits quietly" = "יושב בשקט" (anthropomorphizing the vehicle, as in English). "Lush growth" = "צמחייה שופעת" (abundant vegetation). "All around" = "מסביבך" (around you).

**Term verification:**
- shuttle → רכב הסעות (SQ1: "shuttle craft" = "רכב הסעות")
- platform → משטח נחיתה (SQ1: "platform" = "משטח")

## Message 7
**Original:** "After touching down on a giant landing platform, you are ushered to a hovercraft waiting to transport you to the mining site. Utter despair sets in."

**Translation:** "אחרי נחיתה על משטח נחיתה ענק, אתה מובל לרחפת שמחכה להסיע אותך לאתר המכרה. ייאוש מוחלט משתלט עליך."

**Decision:** Dramatic narrative with dark humor ending. "Touching down" = "נחיתה" (landing - noun form is more natural in Hebrew). "Are ushered" = "אתה מובל" (you are led/conducted). "Hovercraft" = "רחפת" (same as message 1). "Mining site" = "אתר המכרה" (the mine site). "Utter despair sets in" = "ייאוש מוחלט משתלט עליך" (complete despair takes over you) - maintaining the dramatic, sardonic tone.

## Message 8
**Original:** "You recall from when you landed, the entry hatch being on  the rear-left of the shuttle. You'll have to move a little."

**Translation:** "אתה נזכר מהנחיתה, שהצוהר נמצא בחלק האחורי-שמאלי של רכב ההסעות. יהיה עליך לזוז קצת."

**Decision:** Navigation hint. "Hatch" = "צוהר" (SQ1 consistent: "hatch" = "צוהר"). "Rear-left" = "אחורי-שמאלי" (back-left - hyphenated compound). "Shuttle" = "רכב ההסעות" (with definite article). "You'll have to move" = "יהיה עליך לזוז" (literally "it will be upon you to move" - standard Hebrew construction). "A little" = "קצת" (a bit).

**Term verification:**
- hatch → צוהר (SQ1: "hatch" = "צוהר")
- shuttle → רכב הסעות (SQ1: "shuttle craft" = "רכב הסעות")

## Message 9
**Original:** "It looks just like the shuttle that brought you here."

**Translation:** "זה נראה בדיוק כמו רכב ההסעות שהביא אותך לכאן."

**Decision:** Simple observation. "Just like" = "בדיוק כמו" (exactly like). "Shuttle" = "רכב ההסעות" (with definite article - "the shuttle"). "Brought you here" = "שהביא אותך לכאן" (that brought you here).

## Message 10
**Original:** "You open the hatch and head on in."

**Translation:** "אתה פותח את הצוהר ונכנס פנימה."

**Decision:** Action description. "Hatch" = "הצוהר" (the hatch - with definite article). "Head on in" = "נכנס פנימה" (enter inside) - the casual "head on in" becomes the more natural Hebrew "נכנס פנימה".

## Message 11
**Original:** "Through the heavily tinted viewport you see two empty pilot seats."

**Translation:** "דרך הצוהר הכהה אתה רואה שני מושבי טייס ריקים."

**Decision:** Description of what's visible. "Heavily tinted viewport" = "הצוהר הכהה" (the dark/tinted viewport). Using "צוהר" (viewport/porthole) rather than creating a new term. "Tinted" becomes "כהה" (dark/darkened) - more natural in Hebrew. "Pilot seats" = "מושבי טייס" (pilot seats - construct state). "Empty" = "ריקים" (empty - plural masculine).

**Note:** Could have used "חלון" (window) but "צוהר" better fits sci-fi spacecraft terminology and is consistent with SQ1.

## Message 12
**Original:** "You don't have a clear view from here."

**Translation:** "אין לך שדה ראייה ברור מכאן."

**Decision:** Standard "can't see" message. "Clear view" = "שדה ראייה ברור" (clear field of vision). Using the more technical "שדה ראייה" rather than just "מבט" adds a slight sci-fi flavor. "From here" = "מכאן".

---

## Message 13
**Original:** "You can't see the forest for the trees."

**Translation:** "אתה לא רואה את היער מרוב עצים."

**Decision:** This is an English idiom meaning you're missing the big picture because of details. Hebrew has an equivalent idiom: "לא רואה את היער מרוב עצים" (literally: "not seeing the forest from abundance of trees"). Perfect direct translation that maintains both the literal meaning and the idiomatic sense. In context, Roger is probably standing too close to trees to see the wider forest.

---

## Message 14
**Original:** "Things look small down there."

**Translation:** "דברים נראים קטנים שם למטה."

**Decision:** Simple observation from height. Direct translation works perfectly. "Things" = "דברים", "look small" = "נראים קטנים", "down there" = "שם למטה".

---

## Message 15
**Original:** "The sky is quite clear and blue."

**Translation:** "השמיים די בהירים וכחולים."

**Decision:** Atmospheric description. "Quite" = "די" (quite/rather - casual). "Clear" = "בהירים" (clear/bright - using plural because "שמיים" is grammatically plural). "Blue" = "כחולים" (blue - plural). Simple, direct, maintains the peaceful tone.

**Note:** Could have used "צלולים" (clear/lucid) but "בהירים" (bright/clear) is more common in modern Hebrew and works better with "blue".

---

## Overall Translation Strategy

1. **Tone Consistency:** Maintained Space Quest's signature sarcastic, darkly humorous voice throughout
2. **Technical Terms:** Used SQ1-established terminology for all sci-fi/spacecraft terms (shuttle, hatch, platform, guard)
3. **Hebrew Naturalness:** Avoided overly literal translations in favor of idiomatic Hebrew that sounds natural
4. **Cultural Adaptation:** The forest/trees idiom works perfectly in Hebrew; other messages use Israeli Hebrew phrasing
5. **Character Limit:** All translations are roughly similar length to originals
6. **Death Message Tone:** Messages 1 and 2 maintain the game's tradition of being condescending/sarcastic about player deaths
